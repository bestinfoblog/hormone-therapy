How TRT is performed

Testosterone Replacement Therapy can be provided in many ways. However, first, you must be aware of the most effective treatment for your body, by speaking with medical professionals. TRT is mainly administered through the following techniques:

Injection

Injectable testosterone can be administered twice The second injection occurs after four weeks to create the testosterone. The patient is given an injection every ten weeks to keep testosterone levels in check.

Gels

These gels can be applied at least once per day. It is a great option for treatments with needles and consistent levels of testosterone, with daily dosing. However, this method costs more than injections. You might transfer the gel onto others, including children or spouses, via physical contact. It may be a potential irritation to the children.

Testosterone Pellets

Small testosterone pellets can be implanted underneath your skin or in the hip area and dissolve to release testosterone. This procedure will be repeated every 3 to 6 months. This is beneficial since there is no need to do anything at home, however in the course of the treatment, there is a chance that you be at risk of infection or bleeding.

Oral Medication

Different oral medications can be combined with the TRT to increase its effectiveness. They include anastrozole, tamoxifen, HCG, and clomiphene citrate.
